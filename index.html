Option 1

Spain + Portugal

Milan → Lisbon → Porto → Seville → Madrid → Home
9 Days · Jun 20–28 June 20 — Leave Milan ✈ Jun 29 — Madrid MAD → ATL (connecting)

Atlantic culture, fado and flamenco, wine caves, tapas, Roman history, Islamic architecture, coastal cities. This route moves west to east through the Iberian Peninsula — two days in each city with a relaxed enough pace to actually feel each place.

Flight home note: Delta does not operate a nonstop from Madrid to Atlanta. Your best options are a Delta/Air France codeshare via Paris CDG, or Delta via JFK. Book the earliest morning departure from MAD on June 29. Metro Line 8 to Barajas T4, ~40 min.

Day1Jun 20
Fly Milan → Lisbon | Arrive & Explore
Lisbon, Portugal
⌄
✈Milan MXP → Lisbon LIS — Ryanair/Vueling, ~2.5 hrs, ~€40–60. Book ahead.
Afternoon
Arrive Lisbon: Land at Humberto Delgado Airport. Metro Red Line to Alameda, change to Green Line for Rossio (~30 min, €1.65). Drop bags at hostel.
Miradouro da Graca: Highest viewpoint in Lisbon — 360-degree view over the city’s terracotta rooftops, the Tagus River, and the Christ the King statue. Go at sunset.
Alfama neighborhood: Lisbon’s oldest district built on a hillside. Labyrinthine streets, tiled azulejo facades, laundry on balconies. Walk without a plan — the whole quarter is the attraction.
Evening
Fado music: Alfama is the home of fado — Portugal’s melancholic soul music. Taberna da Rua das Flores or A Baiuca for authentic small-room fado with dinner. Book ahead.
Fado meaning: The word means “fate.” The music is about longing, loss, and the sea. Even without understanding Portuguese, it hits differently live in a candlelit tavern.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Bacalhau (salt cod) — Portugal’s national obsession, 365 recipes. Try bacalhau a bras (shredded cod with eggs and potatoes).
›
Pasteis de nata — custard tarts from Pasteis de Belem (the original since 1837). Warm, with cinnamon and powdered sugar.
›
Ginjinha — sweet cherry liqueur in a tiny chocolate cup at A Ginjinha bar in Rossio Square. €1.50.
›
Vinho verde — young, slightly sparkling Portuguese white wine. Perfect for warm June evenings.
Day2Jun 21
Lisbon Full Day
Lisbon, Portugal
⌄
Morning
Pasteis de Belem: Take tram 15E to Belem district. The original custard tart bakery (open since 1837) — queue outside, worth every minute. Order 3, eat them warm with cinnamon.
Torre de Belem: 16th-century fortress on the Tagus River. UNESCO World Heritage — symbol of Portugal’s Age of Exploration. The exterior and river views are the point.
Jeronimos Monastery: Portugal’s most spectacular building. Manueline Gothic architecture built with spice route wealth. The cloister is extraordinary — carved stone that looks like coral.
Afternoon
LX Factory: Former industrial complex turned creative hub. Sunday market with food stalls, vintage clothing, books, and local art. The perfect Lisbon afternoon.
Tram 28: Lisbon’s famous yellow tram winding through the hills, Alfama, and the city center. Genuinely the best way to see the city’s geography.
Miradouro de Santa Catarina: Less touristy viewpoint popular with locals — guitarists, wine, sunset. Far more relaxed than official viewpoints.
Evening
Bairro Alto nightlife: Lisbon’s main bar district. Tiny bars spill onto the streets at night. No cover charge anywhere — you walk between them. Some of Europe’s most accessible nightlife.
Pink Street (Rua Nova do Carvalho): The famous pink-painted street lined with bars. Busy from 10pm, electric by midnight.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Piri-piri chicken (Bonjardim restaurant is the classic), arroz de marisco (seafood rice), or a bifana (pork sandwich).
›
Craft beer — Dois Corvos is Portugal’s best craft brewery. Their taproom is in Intendente neighborhood.
›
Ginjinha and Imperial (draft beer in small glasses) — the two drinks you order standing at a bar.
Day3Jun 22
Lisbon → Porto by Train
Porto, Portugal
⌄
🚇Lisbon Santa Apolonia → Porto Campanha — Alfa Pendular, ~3 hrs, €25–40. Book at cp.pt.
Morning Option
Sintra (optional): 1 hour by train from Rossio. Fairytale palace town in pine-forested hills. Pena Palace (colorful Romanticist hilltop castle), Quinta da Regaleira (mysterious gardens with initiation wells). Leave by noon to catch the Porto train.
Afternoon
Livraria Lello: The world-famous bookshop with a curved red Art Nouveau staircase — inspiration for Harry Potter. Buy a ticket (€5, redeemable against purchase) to avoid the queue.
Dom Luis I Bridge: Iron double-deck bridge over the Douro. Walk the upper deck for views over Porto and Vila Nova de Gaia. The panorama at golden hour is one of the best in Portugal.
Ribeira district: Porto’s colorful riverside neighborhood — UNESCO World Heritage. Walk the Cais da Ribeira quay, have coffee in a riverside cafe.
Evening
Vila Nova de Gaia wine caves: Cross the Dom Luis bridge. Lodge caves of every famous port wine house (Taylor’s, Graham’s, Sandeman) offer tours and tastings for €10–15. Go before 7pm.
Gaia cable car: Gondola from the riverbank up to the upper bridge level. Best sunset view in Porto.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Francesinha — Porto’s signature sandwich. Bread, ham, sausage, steak, covered in melted cheese and spicy tomato-beer sauce, topped with a fried egg. Cafe Santiago is the institution.
›
Port wine — dry white and tawny served chilled at the caves. Not just the sweet red you might know.
›
Pasteis de Chaves — flaky meat pastries from the north. Street food, €1 each.
Day4Jun 23
Porto Full Day
Porto, Portugal
⌄
Morning
Bolhao Market: Porto’s famous restored covered iron market. Fresh fish, vegetables, cheese, flowers. Best 8–11am. Have a coffee and pastel de nata inside.
Majestic Cafe: Porto’s most ornate cafe, open since 1921. Belle Epoque interior — gold mirrors, carved wood, marble. Order a galao (milky coffee) and sit for an hour.
Se Cathedral: Porto’s oldest building, 12th century, on the highest point of the old city. The cloister has azulejo tile panels. The views from the terrace are excellent.
Afternoon
Douro Valley day trip (optional): 1.5 hrs by train to Pinhao. The terraced wine valley is one of Europe’s most beautiful landscapes. Take the Linha do Douro scenic train and return by evening.
Foz do Douro: Tram 1 (Porto’s historic tram line) to where the Douro meets the Atlantic. Walk the rocky coastline, swim at Praia de Matosinhos (Porto’s best beach).
Street Art Quarter: Bonfim and Miragaia neighborhoods have major murals by international artists. World-class street art hidden on every corner.
Evening
Porto nightlife: Rua Galeria de Paris and Rua Candido dos Reis — Porto’s bar streets. Less intense than Lisbon, more local feeling. Go at 10pm.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Bacalhau com natas — salt cod gratin with cream and potato. One of Portugal’s comfort food masterpieces.
›
Matosinhos seafood — Porto’s fishing district. Freshest grilled fish in Portugal, at tables on the street.
›
Super Bock beer — Porto’s local lager, on tap everywhere. Pairs with everything.
Day5Jun 24
Porto → Seville
Seville, Spain
⌄
✈Porto OPO → Seville SVQ — Vueling or Iberia, ~1.5 hrs, ~€40–70. No direct train — flight is the best option.
Afternoon
Real Alcazar: The royal palace complex, still a working royal residence. Stunning Mudejar and Gothic architecture, labyrinthine gardens, ornate tilework. UNESCO World Heritage. €14.50, book online.
Seville Cathedral: The world’s largest Gothic cathedral. Climb the Giralda tower (originally a minaret) for rooftop views. Columbus’s tomb is inside. €13.
Barrio Santa Cruz: Seville’s old Jewish quarter — whitewashed walls, orange trees, flower-filled courtyards, narrow lanes. Get lost in it.
Evening
Tapas crawl: Bar Eslava, El Rinconcillo (oldest bar in Seville, 1670), and La Brunilda are classics. Order at the bar, standing — that’s the way.
Flamenco show: Tablao El Arenal or Casa de la Memoria. Raw, percussive, passionate. Book ahead. ~€35–45.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Jamon iberico de bellota — acorn-fed Iberian ham. Order a platter anywhere.
›
Gazpacho — cold tomato soup drunk as a drink in Andalusia. Refreshing in June heat.
›
Fino sherry (Jerez) — bone-dry fortified wine served cold. The local drink of Andalusia. €2–3 a glass.
›
Espinacas con garbanzos — spinach and chickpea stew. Seville’s iconic tapa, Moorish in origin.
Day6Jun 25
Seville Full Day
Seville, Spain
⌄
Morning
Plaza de Espana: Built for the 1929 Ibero-American Exposition — a vast semicircular palace with tiled alcoves representing every Spanish province. Hire a rowboat on the canal. Free entry.
Parque de Maria Luisa: Spain’s oldest public park. Shady paths, peacocks, fountains. The best place to be in Seville during the June heat.
Torre del Oro: 13th-century golden tower on the Guadalquivir River. Walk the riverside Paseo de Colon.
Afternoon
Metropol Parasol (Las Setas): The world’s largest wooden structure — giant mushroom-shaped canopy in the center of the old city. Walk the elevated walkway (€3) for rooftop city views.
Siesta culture: Seville in late June hits 35°C+ by 2pm. From 2–5pm, the city closes. This is not optional — join the siesta. Eat a long lunch in a shaded courtyard restaurant.
Evening
Golden hour Seville: At 7pm the city reopens and the light turns golden. Walk from the Alcazar to the Cathedral to the river.
Vermut hour: From 7–9pm, Sevillanos drink vermouth with olives at terrace bars. This is pre-dinner — dinner isn’t until 10pm.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Pescaito frito — battered and fried small fish. Seville’s street food. Best in a paper cone at the mercado.
›
Carrillada — braised pig cheeks in red wine. One of Seville’s finest tapas.
›
Rebujito — fino sherry + 7Up over ice. The Seville party drink.
Day7Jun 26
Seville → Madrid by AVE
Madrid, Spain
⌄
🚇Seville Santa Justa → Madrid Puerta de Atocha — AVE high-speed, ~2.5 hrs, €30–70. Spain’s best train route.
Morning
Last Seville morning: Morning walk along the Guadalquivir river before the heat. Mercado de Triana for breakfast.
Afternoon in Madrid
Prado Museum: One of the world’s five greatest art museums. Goya’s Black Paintings, Velazquez’s Las Meninas, El Greco, Raphael, Titian. Book online, free 6–8pm daily. Allow 2–3 hours.
Retiro Park: Madrid’s great park, 350 acres. Rowboat on the lake, Crystal Palace pavilion, rose garden in full bloom in late June. Free entry.
Evening
Malasana neighborhood: Madrid’s hippest barrio. Street art, craft beer bars, record shops, vintage clothing. Evening starts at 8pm and doesn’t end until 5am.
Dinner at 9:30pm: Casa Lucio for huevos rotos (fried eggs over crispy potatoes) — Madrid’s most famous dish.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Cocido madrileño — slow-cooked chickpea stew, served in three courses. Madrid’s defining dish.
›
Bocadillo de calamares — fried squid ring sandwich. Madrid’s most iconic street food. €3–4.
›
Churros con chocolate — Chocolateria San Gines is open 24 hours.
Day8Jun 27
Madrid Full Day
Madrid, Spain
⌄
Morning
Reina Sofia Museum: Picasso’s Guernica in person — always more powerful than the reproductions. The full painting is enormous. Dali, Miro. Free 7–9pm. €12 day entry.
Thyssen-Bornemisza: The third of Madrid’s “Golden Triangle” of museums. Covers Dürer to Hopper to Lichtenstein. Often the least crowded and most varied.
Afternoon
Mercado de San Miguel: Upscale glass-and-iron covered market near Plaza Mayor. Gourmet tapas, jamon carved at the counter, wine, oysters, croquetas.
Gran Via: Madrid’s Broadway — early 20th-century architecture, theatres, flagship stores. Walk the full length from Cibeles to Plaza de Espana.
Azotea del Circulo de Bellas Artes: Best rooftop view in Madrid. €5 entry. 360 degrees over the city. Go at 7pm for golden hour.
Evening
La Latina bar crawl: Calle Cava Baja is lined with traditional mesones. Order media racion (half portion) at each stop and work your way down.
Madrid nightlife: Clubs don’t open until 2am. Kapital (7-floor mega-club), Teatro Barcelo, or Joy Eslava. Madrid is the last night of Europe — go hard.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Jamon iberico de bellota — the best ham in the world, from pigs that eat only acorns.
›
Patatas bravas — fried potatoes with spicy bravas sauce and aioli. Every bar in Madrid has them.
›
Horchata de chufa — chilled tiger nut milk from street kiosks in summer. Refreshing and unique.
Day9Jun 28
Madrid — Final Day | Flight Home Jun 29
Madrid / Barajas MAD
⌄
Final Day Options
Toledo day trip: 30 min by high-speed train from Atocha. Medieval walled city on a hilltop — former capital of the Spanish Empire. Cathedral, synagogues, mosques, and El Greco’s house all within walking distance. UNESCO World Heritage.
Bernabeu Stadium: Real Madrid’s stadium. Museum tour available. Worth a half day if you’re any kind of football fan.
Final Madrid morning: Breakfast at a local bar — cafe solo (espresso) and a tostada con tomate y aceite (toast with tomato and olive oil). The correct Madrid morning ritual.
✈ Flight Home — June 29 from MAD
Delta from Madrid: No nonstop to ATL. Best options: Delta/Air France codeshare via Paris CDG, or Delta via JFK. Flight time ~11–13 hrs total with connection. Book the earliest morning flight out of MAD.

Barajas Airport: Madrid Barajas T4 serves SkyTeam (Delta/Air France). Metro Line 8 from city center, ~40 min, €5.
🍽 Final Bites
›
Tortilla espanola (Spanish omelette) at a bar near your hostel — the definitive Spanish breakfast tapa.
›
Take home: Chorizo, Manchego cheese from Mercado de San Miguel. All airline-legal if vacuum packed.
›
Last drink: Order a clara (lager with lemon) at an outdoor terrace on Gran Via and watch Madrid go by.
Option 2

Croatia + Greece

Milan → Split → Dubrovnik → Athens → ATL
9 Days · Jun 20–28 June 20 — Leave Milan ✈ Jun 29 — Delta NONSTOP Athens ATH → ATL

Roman palaces, Adriatic coast and islands, medieval walled cities, ancient Greek monuments, rooftop bars with 2,500-year-old views. This route delivers the complete Mediterranean experience — Croatian coast in the first half, ancient Greece in the second.

Flight home advantage: Delta operates the only nonstop flight between Athens (ATH) and Atlanta (ATL), ~12 hours. A morning departure from Athens arrives in Atlanta the same afternoon — time zones work in your favor flying west. Book this flight as early as possible.

Day1Jun 20
Fly Milan → Split | Arrive & First Night
Split, Croatia
⌄
✈Milan MXP → Split SPU — Ryanair/easyJet, ~1 hr, ~€30–60. Direct route, book ahead.
Afternoon
Diocletian’s Palace: A 1,700-year-old Roman emperor’s retirement palace covering 30,000 square meters — and an entire medieval city grew up inside it. People still live within the ancient walls today. Walk in through the Golden Gate, find the Cathedral, wander the underground chambers.
Peristyle: The central courtyard of the palace — the Cathedral (converted from the emperor’s mausoleum), a Jupiter temple, and a cafe all share the same ancient Roman space. Order a coffee and sit among 1,700 years of history.
Cathedral of Saint Domnius: The world’s oldest Catholic cathedral, built inside a Roman emperor’s mausoleum. Climb the medieval bell tower for views over the palace rooftops and the Adriatic.
Evening
Riva promenade: Split’s seafront esplanade — lined with palm trees and outdoor bars, with the palace wall on one side and the harbor on the other. The evening passeggiata happens here every night.
First Dalmatian meal: Order grilled fish (riba na zaru) with Swiss chard (blitva) and potatoes. Simple, fresh, the best version of simple food.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Grilled Adriatic fish — branzino, orada, zubatac. Order by the kilo at a konoba. Ask what was caught that morning.
›
Prsut — Dalmatian dry-cured ham, smokier than prosciutto. Every meal starts with prsut and paski sir (Pag island cheese).
›
Salata od hobotnice — octopus salad with olive oil, garlic, and parsley. The definitive Croatian appetizer.
›
Bijelo vino — Posip and Grk white wines from Korcula island. Order a carafe of house white anywhere.
Day2Jun 21
Split Full Day + Hvar Island
Split / Hvar, Croatia
⌄
⛵Split Ferry Terminal → Hvar Town — Catamaran, ~1 hr, €7–12 each way. Last boat back ~9pm.
Morning in Split
Marjan Hill: Pine-covered peninsula above Split. Walk or hike to the hilltop viewpoints (30–45 min up) for panoramic views of the city, the palace below, and the islands. Small chapels cut into the cliff face.
Bacvice Beach: Split’s city beach 10 minutes from the palace. Famous for picigin — a shallow-water ball game invented here. Good swimming.
Afternoon — Hvar Island
Hvar Fortress (Fortica): 20-minute walk uphill from the harbor. Clifftop fortress with extraordinary views over the harbor, the old town, and the Pakleni Islands scattered in the sea below.
Pakleni Islands: Water taxi from the harbor (€5 each way) to small car-free islands with secluded swimming coves and beach bars. Palmizana for developed; Stipanska for quieter swimming.
Evening
Hvar nightlife: Carpe Diem beach club is one of Croatia’s most famous. Optional overnight on Hvar if the vibe calls for it. Or return to Split on the last catamaran.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Gregada — Hvar’s traditional fish stew with potatoes, olive oil, onion, and white wine. The definitive Hvar dish.
›
Hvar lavender honey — the island’s hillsides are covered in lavender. Buy a jar at the harbor market.
›
Skampi na buzaru — prawns in white wine and garlic sauce. Order at a Hvar seafood restaurant over the water.
Day3Jun 22
Split → Dubrovnik by Ferry
Dubrovnik, Croatia
⌄
⛵Split Ferry Terminal → Dubrovnik — Jadrolinija ferry, ~4.5 hrs, €20–35. Sit on the upper deck — the coast is the journey. Or Krilo catamaran (~3 hrs via Hvar and Korcula, ~€30).
Morning — Coastal Ferry
The ferry itself is an experience: The Adriatic coast from Split to Dubrovnik passes dozens of forested islands, old Venetian towns, and cliff-lined coves. Sit on the upper deck with sunscreen. One of the great scenic journeys in Europe.
Korcula Island (catamaran stop): If taking the Krilo catamaran, it stops at Korcula — a small fortified city said to be the birthplace of Marco Polo. The old town is stunning from the water.
Afternoon in Dubrovnik
City Walls: Walk the 2km circuit of medieval walls immediately — before heat and crowds. The walls are 25 meters high in places, with the Adriatic on one side and red-roofed old city on the other. €35 entry — expensive but non-negotiable.
Stradun: The marble-paved main street of the Old Town. Polished by centuries of footsteps — it literally glows in the sunlight. One of the most beautiful streets in Europe.
Fort Lovrijenac: Stands on a cliff outside the city walls — this is the Red Keep from Game of Thrones. Extraordinary views back over the city.
Evening
Buza Bar: A bar cut into the city walls, directly overhanging the Adriatic. Concrete platforms, rope ladders into the sea, cold drinks. The most uniquely located bar in Europe. Follow the “Cold Drinks” signs along the walls.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Black risotto (crni rizot) — risotto made with cuttlefish ink. The defining Croatian coastal dish. Rich, savory, turns your mouth black.
›
Dingac wine — Croatia’s most prestigious red wine, from the Peljesac peninsula. Full-bodied, intense.
›
Rozata — Dubrovnik’s version of crème caramel, flavored with rožata liqueur and rose water. The local dessert tradition.
Day4Jun 23
Dubrovnik Full Day
Dubrovnik, Croatia
⌄
Morning
Old Town at 7am: Walk the Stradun before the cruise ships dock (they arrive by 9am). The early morning light on the walls and streets is extraordinary, and you have it to yourself.
Franciscan Monastery: One of the world’s oldest continuously operating pharmacies (since 1317) is inside. The cloister is beautiful. €5.
Cable Car to Mount Srd: Rises to 412m above the old city. The best panoramic view of Dubrovnik’s walls, harbor, and the islands. €20 return.
Afternoon
Lokrum Island: 15-minute ferry from the Old Town port (€5 return). Car-free island with a botanical garden, peacocks wandering freely, a Benedictine monastery, rocky swimming coves, and a saltwater lake.
Game of Thrones tour: Dubrovnik was King’s Landing. Fort Lovrijenac (Red Keep), Rector’s Palace (Small Council chambers), Pile Gate steps (Sept of Baelor). Maps at tourist offices — walk it independently.
Banje Beach: Best beach in Dubrovnik, just outside the Pile Gate. Afternoon swim with the city walls as backdrop.
Evening
Sunset from the walls: Walk the city walls at golden hour. The Adriatic light at sunset on Dubrovnik’s rooftops is one of the most photographed moments in Europe.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Seafood platter (misto plate) — fish, squid, mussels, prawns, all grilled over charcoal. Order for two.
›
Posip white wine from Korcula — Croatia’s finest white wine. Crisp, mineral. Order a half-liter carafe.
›
Stonska torta — a sweet cake with rice, almonds, and raisins from the nearby town of Ston.
Day5Jun 24
Dubrovnik → Athens
Athens, Greece
⌄
✈Dubrovnik DBV → Athens ATH — Aegean Airlines or Croatia Airlines, ~1.5 hrs, ~€50–90. Multiple daily flights.
Morning
Final Dubrovnik morning: Walk the old town one last time before 9am. Buy sea salt from Ston, Dingac wine, or Croatian olive oil at the Gunduliceva Poljana morning market.
Afternoon in Athens
Arrive Athens: Metro from Athens airport (Line 3, blue line) to Monastiraki or Syntagma square — ~45 min, €10.50. Buy a 3-day transit pass.
Monastiraki neighborhood: The old bazaar district at the foot of the Acropolis. Flea market, street food, cafes with Acropolis views, energy everywhere.
Acropolis first view from Philopappou Hill: Don’t go up to the Acropolis today. Instead walk to Philopappou Hill (free, 15 min from Monastiraki) at dusk. The Parthenon lit up on its rock as the sun sets is the best first glimpse.
Anafiotika: A tiny neighborhood built into the Acropolis rock by Cycladic islanders — whitewashed walls, bougainvillea, cats. Feels like you’ve teleported to a Greek island in the middle of a capital city.
Evening
First Greek meal: Order a spread of mezedes — tzatziki, taramasalata (fish roe dip), saganaki (fried cheese), grilled octopus, dolmades, and a carafe of house wine. This is the correct first Athens meal.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Souvlaki — skewered pork or chicken in pita with tzatziki, tomato, onion. Monastiraki Square has the best spots. €2.50–3.
›
Spanakopita — spinach and feta in phyllo pastry. Every bakery sells it fresh in the morning. €1.50.
›
Ouzo — anise-flavored spirit that turns cloudy when water is added. Drink with ice and mezedes. Ouzo and seafood is the correct pairing.
Day6Jun 25
Athens — Acropolis + Ancient City
Athens, Greece
⌄
Morning — Go Early
Acropolis at 8am opening: Arrive at the main Dionysiou Areopagitou entrance at 8am exactly. You have ~90 minutes before heat and crowds make it significantly less enjoyable. The Parthenon, Erechtheion (porch of the Caryatids), Propylaea, and Temple of Athena Nike. €20 entry.
Acropolis Museum: Directly below the Acropolis. The Parthenon Gallery reconstructs the full frieze. Original sculptures the British Museum won’t return are displayed here, with plaster copies where the originals are. €10.
Afternoon
National Archaeological Museum: The most important collection of ancient Greek art in the world. The Antikythera Mechanism (world’s oldest known analog computer, 2,000 years old), the Mask of Agamemnon, the Bronze Poseidon/Zeus. Allow 2–3 hours.
Syntagma Square: Changing of the Evzone guard at the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier. The Evzones wear distinctive pleated kilts and pom-pom shoes. Ceremony every hour.
Plaka neighborhood: Athens’ most picturesque district — Orthodox churches, small museums, and the best gyros stands in the city.
Evening
Rooftop bars: Athens has extraordinary rooftop bars with direct Acropolis views. A for Athens, Couleur Locale, and Hotel Grande Bretagne’s rooftop all have the view. Order a Greek wine and watch the Acropolis glow at night.
Dinner in Psiri or Monastiraki: Tzitzikas kai Mermingas for modern Greek, or Diporto Agoras (hidden basement taverna with no menu — they bring you what’s cooked that day).
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Lamb kleftiko — lamb slow-cooked in parchment with herbs and lemon. Named after Greek mountain bandits who cooked stolen lamb in underground ovens.
›
Moussaka — layers of eggplant, minced meat, and bechamel. The definitive Greek comfort food.
›
Baklava — layers of phyllo, honey, and chopped nuts. Athens has serious baklava shops worth finding.
›
Freddo espresso — Greece invented cold coffee. Espresso shaken with ice. You will drink 2 per day in June heat.
Day7Jun 26
Athens — Day Trip or Greek Islands
Athens / Cape Sounion / Aegina
⌄
Option A — Cape Sounion
Temple of Poseidon: Bus from Athens (~90 min each way, €7 return). A clifftop Ancient Greek temple at the southernmost tip of Attica, 70m above the Aegean. Lord Byron carved his name in a column in 1810. Sunset here is one of the great views in Greece. €10 entry.
Option B — Aegina Island
Aegina: 1-hour hydrofoil from Piraeus (Metro accessible). Small island famous for pistachios. Temple of Aphaia — well-preserved 5th-century BC temple. Swim at the beaches, eat fresh fish, return by evening.
Option C — Athens Neighborhoods
Exarcheia: Athens’ radical/arts neighborhood. Street art on every surface, record shops, cheap food, unconventional bars. A side of Athens most tourists never see.
Kerameikos: Ancient cemetery at the edge of the Agora. Far less visited than the Acropolis but atmospheric — grave monuments, sacred road, the ancient city gate.
🍽 Local Cuisine
›
Pistachios from Aegina — buy directly from a farm stall. Genuinely different from imported pistachios.
›
Horta — wild greens (dandelion, chicory) boiled and dressed with olive oil and lemon. Every taverna has them.
›
Strapatsada — eggs cooked in tomato sauce with feta. A perfect lunch at a neighborhood cafe.
Day8Jun 27
Athens — Final Full Day
Athens, Greece
⌄
Morning
Athens Central Market (Varvakios Agora): The meat and fish halls of Athens’ central market — visceral, real, extraordinary. The surrounding streets have olive oil, spice, cheese, and wine shops. Buy provisions here.
Ancient Agora: The marketplace and civic center of ancient Athens — where Socrates taught and democracy was debated. The Temple of Hephaestus is the best-preserved ancient Greek temple in the world (better than the Parthenon). Beautiful site with fewer crowds. €10.
Hadrian’s Library and Arch: Roman emperor Hadrian’s gifts to Athens — a monumental arch marking the boundary between the Greek old city and his Roman new city. Free to view from outside.
Afternoon
Stavros Niarchos Cultural Center: Designed by Renzo Piano on the coast south of Athens (metro accessible). The rooftop gives unexpected views of the Saronic Gulf. The park, library, and opera house are all worth exploring.
Sunset from Lycabettus Hill: The highest point in Athens (277m). Take the funicular or walk up. 360-degree panoramic view over Athens to the sea. The Acropolis is visible in the middle of the city below. Arrive 30 minutes before sunset.
Evening — Last Night in Europe
Gazi neighborhood: Athens’ modern nightlife hub near the old gasworks. Bar-hop from Bios to Gazaki to Noel. Starts around 10pm and runs until 5am.
Final Greek feast: Go somewhere special tonight — Spondi (two Michelin stars, Greek nouvelle cuisine) or Hytra (one star). Or Karamanlidika tou Fani — a deli-taverna serving charcuterie, cheese, and mezedes from Greek minority communities. Fascinating food and a great final meal.
