Jerónimos · Torre de Belém · Time Out
›
⛪
Jerónimos Monastery
Portugal's greatest architectural treasure — a UNESCO World Heritage Site built in 1496. The two-story Manueline cloister is extraordinary: every arch carved with ropes, coral, and maritime symbols. Vasco da Gama is buried here. Book timed entry online in advance — sells out in peak season. Cloister entry €10; church is free. Open Tue–Sun 9:30am–5:30pm.
⚡ Official Tickets
🗼
Torre de Belém
A 16th-century tower rising from the Tagus River, built to defend Lisbon's harbor. Its ornate Manueline exterior — sea monsters, knots, armillary spheres — is one of Portugal's iconic images. Short walk along the riverfront from Jerónimos. Entry ~€6.
🎟 Map to Torre de Belém
🍽
Time Out Market
Lisbon's legendary food hall in an 1892 iron market building. Dozens of the city's best chefs serve side by side — pastéis de nata, grilled fish, bifanas, wine. No booking needed; grab a tray and try everything.

Sintra Day Trip
›
🚂
CP Train from Rossio Station
Depart from Rossio in central Lisbon — trains every 20–30 min, ~40 min journey, €2.30 RT. Use your Viva Viagem card. Leave before 9am — Sintra is packed by 10:30am in summer.
Map Train Route
🏰
Pena Palace
A wildly colorful Romanticist palace perched on a misty hilltop — candy-striped towers, medieval battlements, and Moorish arches crammed together. Built in the 1840s for King Ferdinand II. One of Europe's most surreal sights. Timed entry required; sells out days in advance in summer. Book now.
⚡ GetYourGuide
🌿
Quinta da Regaleira
A neo-Gothic estate with mystical gardens hiding grottos, tunnels, and the famous Initiation Well — a 27-meter spiral staircase descending into the earth. Built by an eccentric millionaire obsessed with Freemasonry and Tarot. Less crowded than Pena and arguably more memorable.

Barrio Santa Cruz — Evening Wander
Seville's medieval Jewish quarter: a labyrinth of whitewashed alleyways barely wide enough for two people, draped in bougainvillea and smelling of orange blossom. Get lost on purpose. Stop at any tapas bar — order jamón, salmorejo, cold Cruzcampo. The best way to arrive in Seville.

Alcázar · Cathedral · Flamenco
›
🏯
Real Alcázar
The oldest royal palace still in use in Europe — the Spanish royal family still stays here. A dizzying mix of Mudéjar tile-work, geometric ceilings, sunlit patios, and shaded gardens built across a thousand years. Book timed entry well ahead — June dates sell out weeks in advance. Strict 30-min entry window; don't be late. Entry €15.50, opens 9:30am.
⚡ Official Site 🎟 Buy Tickets
⛪
Seville Cathedral + Giralda Tower
The largest Gothic cathedral in the world, built on a 12th-century mosque — its minaret (the Giralda) still stands. Climb via ramps (no stairs!) for unforgettable panoramic views of Seville. Christopher Columbus is buried inside. Entry €13 online. Book ahead; peak slots sell out. Opens 10:45am.
⚡ Official Tickets
💃
Flamenco — Evening Show
Seville is the birthplace of flamenco. Skip touristy dinner shows — find a smaller tablao or bar. The stomping, clapping, wailing guitar and raw emotion are unforgettable even for 30 minutes. Ask your hostel for a local recommendation, or book below.

Plaza de España — Morning Visit
A sweeping semi-circular plaza built for the 1929 Ibero-American Exposition — grand colonnade, ornate bridges, 48 ceramic tile alcoves representing each Spanish province. Rent a rowboat on the canal. One of the most beautiful public spaces in Europe. Free entry; best before 11am.

Toledo — City of Three Cultures
A medieval city on a granite hill, once home to Christians, Muslims, and Jews living together. Cobblestone streets lead to the Gothic Cathedral, the Alcázar fortress, and the El Greco Museum. Get lost in the alleys — Toledo rewards the curious. Half-day is enough; full day is better.

👑
Royal Palace of Madrid
The largest palace in Western Europe — 3,478 rooms of jaw-dropping Italian Baroque excess built by Philip V in the 1700s. The official royal residence until 1931; today open to the public. Don't miss the Throne Room, the Hall of Mirrors, the porcelain room, and works by Velázquez, Goya, and Caravaggio. Entry €14 online. Free Mon–Thu 5–7pm in summer for non-EU visitors if you don't mind a queue. Allow 2–3 hours. Book skip-the-line to avoid a long wait on Saturdays.
⚡ GetYourGuide 🌐 Official Site Map Route
🌅
Temple of Debod — Sunset
A genuine 2,200-year-old Egyptian temple gifted to Spain in 1968, reconstructed in a hilltop park just west of the palace. The view of the Madrid skyline at golden hour from here is one of the best in the city. Free entry to the grounds. Arrive 30–45 min before sunset for the best light and manageable crowds.
🌐 Info
🍻
Malasaña Neighborhood — Evening
Madrid's coolest neighborhood: a former working-class barrio now packed with independent bars, vintage shops, record stores, and street art. Saturday nights here are electric. Wander Calle Fuencarral and Plaza del Dos de Mayo, grab craft beers at any terrace bar, and stay as late as you want — it only gets better after midnight.

🖼
Prado Museum
One of the greatest art museums on earth — Las Meninas (Velázquez), Saturn Devouring His Son (Goya), Garden of Earthly Delights (Bosch). 8,000+ works across 700 years. Go early or at lunch. Entry €15 online. ⚡ Free Mon–Sat 6–8pm & Sun 5–7pm — arrive 45+ min early to queue. Buy timed entry to skip the line.
⚡ Official Tickets
🌆
Azotea del Círculo de Bellas Artes
The best rooftop bar in Madrid — 360° panoramic views from atop a 1920s Art Deco building on Alcalá street. Small entry fee (~€5). Get there around 9:30–10pm for golden hour drinks in June. Arrive early — fills up fast.

Rastro Flea Market · Final Evening
›
🛍
El Rastro Flea Market
Europe's largest open-air flea market through La Latina on Sunday mornings — vintage, antiques, records, oddities. Locals drink vermut in nearby bars from noon. Best before 12pm. ⚠️ Keep bag in front — pickpockets work the crowd. Free to browse.

🥩
Final Dinner in Madrid
Dinner at 9:30–10pm is normal here. Try La Bola (open since 1870) for cocido madrileño stew, or tapas-hop through Calle Cava Baja in La Latina. End the night at a bodega or rooftop bar — Madrid keeps going until 3am.

Lisbon — Viva Viagem Card
Buy at Metro station on arrival
Works on Metro, buses, trams (Tram 28 & 15E), ferries, and the CP train to Sintra. Each person needs their own card. Buy at any Metro ticket machine when you land — card costs €0.50, then load credit.

24h pass~€6.15 (unlimited)

Madrid — Tarjeta Multi
Buy at Atocha when you arrive from Seville
Madrid's rechargeable card for Metro, buses, and light rail. Valid 10 years. One card can serve two people if you load 10-trip tickets — tap each person through the gate one by one. Buy from red-stickered machines at any Metro station.
Card cost€2.50 (one-time, reusable)
10 trips€7.30 — best value for 4 nights
Single ride€1.50–€2.00 (Zone A)
Airport+€3 surcharge on Line 8 to MAD

Seville
🌡
Heat: June avg 35°C+ (95°F). Sightsee before noon and after 6pm. Siesta 2–5pm. Hydrate.
🛴
Transport: Old city is walkable · Lime/Bird scooters · Uber and taxis reliable and cheap
💶
Currency: Euro · Tap-to-pay everywhere including tapas bars
⚠️
Pickpockets: Heavy around Cathedral entrance and Santa Cruz tourist zones
🕘
Culture: Dinner 9–10pm · "Gracias" / "Hola" · Everything moves slower — lean into it
🍺
Drink: Order a Rebujito (sherry + lemon Fanta) — Seville's classic summer drink, €2–3

🚇
